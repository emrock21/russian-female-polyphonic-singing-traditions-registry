# Russian Female Polyphonic Singing Traditions Registry

This repository preserves, in an immutable and decentralized format, the female polyphonic singing traditions of Russia — one of the most complex and regionally diverse vocal heritages in the world.

Russian female polyphony includes powerful open-throat “white voice” singing, dense multi-voice harmonies, drone-based structures, ritual laments, and lyrical drawn-out melodies. Each region developed its own vocal technique, modal system, and ritual context.

## Why Russian Female Polyphony Matters

These traditions represent:

- a **unique vocal technique**, including open-throat singing and dense harmonies  
- a **regional musical diversity**, with Belgorod, Bryansk, Kursk, Don, and Smolensk schools  
- a **ritual function**, tied to weddings, harvest, funerals, and seasonal rites  
- a **linguistic heritage**, preserving archaic dialects and phonetics  
- an **endangered cultural system**, maintained by small village ensembles  

It is one of the richest polyphonic traditions in Eastern Europe.

## Purpose of This Contract

The goal of this registry is to:

1. **Create a permanent, censorship-resistant record** of Russian female polyphonic traditions.  
2. **Preserve endangered vocal knowledge** with ethnomusicological accuracy.  
3. **Document styles, regions, techniques, and ritual contexts**.  
4. **Provide an immutable reference** for researchers, choirs, and future generations.  
5. **Honor the regional diversity of Russia’s vocal heritage.**

The contract is intentionally:

- **ownerless** — no authority can alter or censor the registry  
- **payment-free** — no monetization, no barriers  
- **minimalistic and secure** — no external calls  
- **permanent** — entries become part of the cultural ledger  

## What Each Entry Records

- **styleName** — Belgorod white voice, Bryansk dense polyphony, Don Cossack style  
- **region** — Belgorod, Bryansk, Kursk, Don, Smolensk, Voronezh  
- **vocalTechnique** — open-throat, dense harmony, drone-based, drawn-out  
- **musicalFeatures** — modal scales, dissonance, parallel intervals  
- **performanceContext** — weddings, harvest, funerals, seasonal rituals  
- **linguisticFeatures** — dialect, phonetics, archaic vocabulary  
- **uniqueness** — regional identity, rare techniques
